# DATA RETENTION AND ARCHIVAL POLICIES

## Data Retention Framework

Almabase maintains a comprehensive data retention policy that balances operational needs with privacy and compliance requirements. All data retention decisions are documented and reviewed annually.

## Retention Periods by Data Type

### Student Records
- Active student records: Maintained for duration of enrollment plus 7 years
- Inactive student records: Retained per institution-defined retention schedule (typically 5-7 years)
- Transcript records: Maintained permanently per FERPA requirements
- Financial aid records: Retained for 7 years minimum

### Donor and Constituent Records
- Active donor records: Maintained indefinitely or per institutional policy
- Inactive donors: Retained for minimum 5 years after last transaction
- Prospect records: Retained for 3 years unless institution requests longer period
- Donation records: Permanent retention with audit trail

### System and Access Logs
- Login and access logs: Retained for 1 year
- Data modification logs: Retained for 7 years for audit compliance
- Security incident logs: Retained for minimum 2 years
- Backup logs: Retained for 90 days

### Backup and Disaster Recovery Data
- Production backups: Retained for 90 days with daily incremental backups
- Archive backups: Retained for 7 years for long-term compliance
- Geo-redundant copies: Maintained in separate geographic regions
- Point-in-time recovery: Supported for 30-day recovery window

## Data Deletion and Archiving

When institutions request deletion of data:
1. Data is marked for deletion and removed from active systems within 7 days
2. Data is removed from all backups within 30 days through secure deletion protocols
3. Verification reports are generated and provided to the institution
4. Deletion is cryptographically verified and logged

### Archive Process
Data selected for archiving is:
- Compressed and encrypted for storage efficiency
- Stored in cold storage reducing operational costs
- Indexed for searchable retrieval if needed
- Maintained with integrity verification checks

## Retention Schedule Reviews

Annual reviews of retention schedules ensure:
- Compliance with evolving regulatory requirements
- Alignment with institutional needs
- Cost optimization of storage resources
- Updated procedures for new data types

## Legal Hold Procedures

When legal holds are issued:
- All targeted data is immediately protected from deletion
- Normal retention schedules are suspended
- Legal hold status is tracked and monitored
- Regular communication with legal counsel on hold status

## Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity

Data retention policies support disaster recovery objectives:
- RPO (Recovery Point Objective): 24 hours maximum data loss
- RTO (Recovery Time Objective): 4 hours for critical systems
- Regular disaster recovery drills test recovery procedures
- Multi-region redundancy ensures no single point of failure
